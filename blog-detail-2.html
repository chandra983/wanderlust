10 Best Beaches in South East Asia

By Michael Chen Jan 20, 2024 6 min read

Crystal clear turquoise waters and soft white sand in El Nido, Philippines.

Table of Contents

    South East Asia is a paradise for beach lovers. From the secluded lagoons of the Philippines to the vibrant shores of Thailand, there is a stretch of sand for every type of traveler. In this guide, we've narrowed down the absolute best spots to soak up the sun.

    1. El Nido, Palawan, Philippines

    Often cited as having the most beautiful scenery in the world, El Nido is famous for its towering limestone karsts and hidden lagoons. The water is so clear you can see the coral reefs from above the surface.

    "Palawan is the last frontier of the Philippines, a place where nature still reigns supreme over modernization."

    2. Railay Beach, Krabi, Thailand

    Accessible only by boat, Railay Beach is a rock climber's paradise and a swimmer's dream. The dramatic cliffs provide a backdrop that is unlike anywhere else in the world.

    Best Time to Visit Railay

    The peak season is from November to March when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the shoulder season (October, April) offers better value and fewer crowds.

    A traditional longtail boat resting on the shores of Krabi, Thailand.

    3. Pink Beach, Komodo Island, Indonesia

    As the name suggests, the sand here has a distinct pink hue due to microscopic organisms called Foraminifera. It's one of only seven pink beaches in the world.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Are these beaches safe for children?
    Yes, most popular resorts have shallow areas, but always be aware of currents and jelly fish seasons.
    Do I need a visa for South East Asia?
    Many nationalities can enter Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines visa-free for short stays, but always check current regulations.
